# Peter's Digest Video Prompt - 2026-03-06

## Story Selection

- **Story:** The Amazon Drone Cover-up
- **Hook:** Amazon blamed a "code deployment" for yesterday’s massive outage, but their data centers were actually being hit by drone strikes.
- **Source:** AI Infrastructure News (Perigon 2)
- **Why it works:** **Social Value** & **High-arousal (Surprise)**. It offers a "wait, what?" moment by connecting a common consumer frustration to a high-stakes geopolitical event.

- **Story:** The $80 Million "Yoink"
- **Hook:** Hungary just seized $80 million in gold and cash from a Ukrainian bank transfer to use as "leverage" for a pipeline repair.
- **Source:** International Intrigue (Hungary)
- **Why it works:** **High-arousal (Awe/Anger)**. It’s an absurd, high-stakes geopolitical heist that feels like a movie plot.

- **Story:** BlackRock’s "Canary in the Coal Mine"
- **Hook:** The world’s biggest asset manager just trapped its investors—BlackRock is limiting redemptions as its flagship private credit fund hits a liquidity wall.
- **Source:** Screening Candidates (BlackRock)
- **Why it works:** **Personal Value (Fear)**. It signals a major shift in the credit cycle and hits investors where it hurts: access to their own cash.

- **Story:** Welcome to the 1970s
- **Hook:** We just lost 92,000 jobs in a single month while oil broke $90—that’s the recipe for the "Stagflation" nightmare.
- **Source:** Market Overview (Stagflation / Jobs Report)
- **Why it works:** **Personal Value** & **Intellectual Novelty**. It makes complex macro data feel urgent and personal by using the "70s Playbook" framing.

## Script

**(Scene 1 — The Shadow over the Van)**
**Narrator:** If you couldn't check out on Amazon yesterday, you were told it was a "code deployment error." But while your app was spinning, actual drones were hitting AWS data centers in the Middle East.

**(Scene 2 — The Falling Dominoes)**
**Narrator:** Facilities in Bahrain and the UAE were targeted in direct strikes. It’s a massive shift: the "Cloud" isn't just software anymore—it’s physical infrastructure that can be touched... and broken.

**(Scene 3 — The Heist)**
**Narrator:** While that’s happening, Hungary just pulled an $80 million "yoink." They seized two armored vans full of gold and cash from a Ukrainian bank, basically holding the money hostage to force a pipeline repair.

**(Scene 4 — The Fishing Pole)**
**Narrator:** But the real "canary in the coal mine" is BlackRock. They just limited redemptions on their flagship private credit fund. When the world’s biggest asset manager tells you that you can’t have your cash back, the credit cycle has officially turned.

**(Scene 5 — The Disco Snail)**
**Narrator:** The big picture? We just lost 92,000 jobs while oil broke $90. It’s an 11-letter word that haunted the 70s: Stagflation. The "Wall of Worry" just got a lot taller—keep your eye on the boring stuff.

## Visual Prompts

### Guidelines
- **Style:** Miniature Model / Diorama.
- **Aesthetic:** Handcrafted textures (clay, wood, paper), tiny figures, and visible craft materials.
- **Consistency:** Every prompt reinforces the small-scale handcrafted physical miniature set aesthetic.

**Scene 1 — The Shadow over the Van**
- **Image Prompt:** A small-scale handcrafted physical miniature set of a quiet suburban street. A tiny blue Amazon delivery van made of painted balsa wood is driving past paper-craft houses. A giant, oversized "Looney Tunes" style black bomb with a lit sparkler fuse is hanging by a thick, visible fishing line directly over the van. Tabletop diorama, visible miniature craft textures, macro photography, soft studio lighting.
- **Video Prompt:** Static shot. The giant bomb slowly lowers further toward the van, its fuse sparkling with tiny light effects.

**Scene 2 — The Falling Dominoes**
- **Image Prompt:** A tabletop diorama representing a server room. The server racks are actually giant black dominoes made of clay, arranged in a long winding line. A tiny toy drone, looking like a miniature scale model, has just nudged the first domino, and they are captured mid-topple. One domino has a tiny hand-painted "AWS" logo. Small-scale handcrafted physical miniature set, visible textures, dramatic high-contrast lighting.
- **Video Prompt:** Slow push-in on the dominoes as they continue to fall in a cascading wave toward the camera.

**Scene 3 — The Heist**
- **Image Prompt:** A miniature mountain pass scene crafted from crumpled brown paper and pebbles. Two tiny, detailed armored bank vans are caught in a giant, oversized butterfly net held by a tiny figure in a colorful Hungarian hussar uniform. The net is made of real mesh and a wooden handle. Tiny handmade miniature scale models, visible craft textures, bright daylight studio lighting.
- **Video Prompt:** The camera pans from the armored vans struggling inside the mesh net up to the triumphant tiny figure holding the handle.

**Scene 4 — The Fishing Pole**
- **Image Prompt:** A close-up of a miniature bank vault door made of dull grey clay. A massive, oversized "OUT OF ORDER" sign is taped across the dial with neon pink duct tape. A tiny man in a business suit is standing on a footstool, trying to fish a single gold bar out of the vault's mail slot using a literal miniature fishing pole. Small-scale handcrafted physical miniature set, moody lighting, visible clay fingerprints.
- **Video Prompt:** The tiny man jiggles the fishing pole, the gold bar hooking onto the line but getting stuck in the narrow slot.

**Scene 5 — The Disco Snail**
- **Image Prompt:** A top-down shot of a miniature desk. A giant, real garden snail is wearing a tiny 1970s-style afro wig and bell-bottoms. The snail is slowly crawling across a miniature stock chart that serves as a disco dance floor, with tiny colorful "tiles" painted on it. The chart is physically "melting" off the desk like soft wax. Tiny figures made as miniature models, soft warm lighting, visible snail trail across the "dance floor."
- **Video Prompt:** Time-lapse shot of the snail slowly pulsing as it moves across the melting chart, the tiny disco wig bobbing.
