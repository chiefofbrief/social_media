# Video Prompt — 2026-03-10

### Story Selection

- **Story:** Amazon’s "Code Deployment" vs. Drone Strikes
  - **Hook:** Amazon blamed a massive outage on a "code deployment error"—while Iranian drones were literally blowing up their data centers in the Middle East.
  - **Source:** AI Ecosystem Positioning / Screening Candidates
  - **Viral Driver:** Social value — It creates an immediate "wait, what?" reaction by exposing a massive gap between corporate PR and physical reality.

- **Story:** BlackRock’s $10 Trillion Liquidity Trap
  - **Hook:** BlackRock—managing $10 trillion—just told investors they can’t have their money back from their flagship private credit fund.
  - **Source:** General Stock News Analysis / Screening Candidates
  - **Viral Driver:** High-arousal emotion — It triggers immediate concern and surprise; it's the "canary in the coal mine" for the broader credit cycle.

- **Story:** The Nike Hiring Mystery
  - **Hook:** Nike stock is down 20% and the vibes are terrible, yet their job postings just surged by 79% in a week.
  - **Source:** Screening Candidates / Reddit r/ValueInvesting
  - **Viral Driver:** Intellectual novelty — It’s a counter-intuitive anomaly that breaks the "dying brand" narrative with hard alternative data.

### Script

**(Scene 0 — Intro)**
**Narrator:** Amazon’s drone-powered "glitch," BlackRock’s $10 trillion piggy bank, and Nike’s secret army of painters.

**(Scene 1 — The Data Center "Glitch")**
**Narrator:** Amazon blamed yesterday’s massive US shopping outage on a "code deployment error"—which is technically true if the code was deployed by an Iranian drone hitting their Middle East data centers. Apparently, the "cloud" is a lot more flammable when it’s located in a conflict zone.

**(Scene 2 — BlackRock's Piggy Bank)**
**Narrator:** BlackRock—managing over $10 trillion—needs a little more time with your money. Their flagship private credit fund just capped redemptions because too many people wanted out at once. It turns out $10 trillion doesn't go as far as it used to when everyone starts looking for the exit.

**(Scene 3 — Nike's Secret Sprints)**
**Narrator:** Nike stock is down 20% and the internet says the brand is dead, but their hiring just surged 79% in a week. Either they’ve found a massive turnaround signal, or they’re hiring an army of people to find where the "swoosh" went. Keep your eye on the boring stuff.

### Visual Prompts

**Guidelines**
The visual style is **Miniature Model / Diorama**. Every scene is a small-scale, handcrafted physical miniature set built on a dark, polished wooden tabletop. Tiny figures are 1:87 scale hand-painted plastic models with visible craft textures and brushstrokes. Lighting is focused and dramatic, using a macro-photography setup with a shallow depth of field to emphasize the "toy-like" scale of global finance.

**Scene 1 — The Data Center "Glitch"**
- **Beginning Frame:** On a dark, polished wooden tabletop, a row of miniature "server racks" made from painted matchboxes and balsa wood blocks. A tiny, roughly-painted plastic drone—supported by a thin, visible silver wire—hovers inches from the servers. High-contrast macro lighting creates long, dramatic shadows across the wood grain.
- **End Frame:** The tiny drone has "crashed" into the matchbox racks, which are scattered like a child's knocked-over blocks. Tiny puffs of white cotton wool "smoke" are glued to the balsa wood wreckage, and tangles of brightly colored embroidery thread "wires" are spilled across the wooden tabletop.
- **Video Prompt:** A fast, low-angle camera push-in toward the servers, capturing the moment the drone hits the balsa wood blocks and scatters the matchboxes in a dramatic, small-scale disaster.

**Scene 2 — BlackRock's Piggy Bank**
- **Beginning Frame:** A massive, hand-molded clay piggy bank, painted a dull charcoal grey with visible brushstrokes, sits in the center of the wooden tabletop. A single tiny plastic man in a navy suit—no bigger than a grain of rice—is leaning his shoulder against the giant clay pig, his feet "slipping" on the polished wood surface.
- **End Frame:** The camera pulls back to reveal a long, winding line of dozens of identical tiny plastic businessmen stretching across the wooden table. They are all posed in a chain, leaning forward and pushing fruitlessly against the giant, immovable clay pig. The edges of the wooden table are blurred in a soft macro-focus.
- **Video Prompt:** A slow, steady camera pan from right to left, traveling from the first man’s individual struggle down the increasingly long and desperate line of tiny figures.

**Scene 3 — Nike's Secret Sprints**
- **Beginning Frame:** A sheet of bright green textured craft felt, cut into the shape of a running track, is laid out on the wooden tabletop. A tiny plastic figure in a track suit holds a miniature paintbrush, frozen mid-stroke as he paints a white "swoosh" onto the felt. The lighting is bright and clinical, emphasizing the texture of the green felt.
- **End Frame:** The camera has pulled back to show a chaotic swarm of fifty tiny figures, all with paintbrushes, frantically painting white logos over every inch of the felt track and even onto the wooden table itself. The logos are overlapping in a messy, hyper-active frenzy of white paint.
- **Video Prompt:** A smooth camera pull-back and tilt-up, transitioning from the single worker’s focused task to the sprawling, manic chaos of the entire miniature workforce painting at a frantic pace.
